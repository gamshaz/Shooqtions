Data was somewhat of an afterthought again with Iran headlines the focus. Thursday’s data however was a mixed bag with PMI’s close to estimates while the wkly claims data show no signs of labor mkt weakening. After jumping back above 4.10% on the earlier Iran headlines, 2y yields drifted back to 4.06% into the close. 1y1y OIS ranged 3.932-4.015 before going out around 3.97%. -Front-end was underperforming on the curve with continued focus on Weds FOMC minutes. Recall, the minutes noted “A majority of participants highlighted…that some policy firming would likely become appropriate if inflation were to continue to run persistently above 2%,” and “many” participants would have preferred removing the implicit easing bias from the post-meeting statement. While the  “vast majority” of participants saw greater risk that inflation would take longer to return to 2% than they had previously expected Follow-through from the minutes kept a lid on the front-end as the curve continued to edge flatter. - US front-end also continued to underperform x-mkt: 1y1y OIS cheapening further vs Europe & UK where rate hike urgency continues to back off the extremes seen in April. Recall, the US/EUR 1y1y spread tightened sharply through early April, trading through +50bps as the mkt was pricing more aggressive policy tightening from the ECB (&BoE) relative the Fed. Since the lows (traded Apr 7th), US 1y1y OIS has underperformed with ECB pricing stabilizing while US mkts started to price more aggressive rate hikes. The US/EUR spread got out to +105bps – the widest since August of last year (taking out local resist at ~99bps)
