## Flow & commentary

The economic calendar feed wasn't available this week, but the ITC commentary tells a clear story: this was a headline-driven week pinned to Iran/oil and capped by Wednesday's hawkish FOMC minutes — no first-tier data print to anchor positioning.

**Monday-Tuesday (the bear-steepener):** ITC flagged "technical selling, inflation concerns drive belly-led rise in yields; 30y highest since 2007." Tuesday saw $12m/01 of block selling in TY/FV; 5y cheapened to 4.35%. The desk flow log matches: paper paid 21 on 20k of SFRZ6 96.00/95.25 put spread and 13 on 21k the same day — that's the print behind the SR3Z6 96.00p +8,966 / 95.75p +8,882 OI builds. SFRU6 95.9375/95.687/95.4375 put fly went up 27k at 1.25, with 7k of that as outright — heavy U6 downside on the day belly cracked.

**Wednesday (the oversold bounce):** ITC: "Iran optimism triggers oversold rebound, belly led." WTI -7% intraday on AlArabiya/Trump headlines about a "final stages" Iran deal. Strip recovered: reds +10 to +12 ticks. Despite the rally, flow was still buying downside: SFRZ6 95.625p paid 9.5 on 7.5k, SFRZ6 96.00/95.25 put spread paid another 12.75 on 10k and 12 on 10k. SFRZ6 96.25/96.75 risk reversal paid 24.25 on 5250 (+puts) — paying for downside skew on the bounce. That lines up with the 0QU6 95.50p +12,830 and 96.00p +12,500 OI moves the same session: protection added through the rally, not faded.

**Thursday (FOMC minutes follow-through):** ITC: "Choppy session overall, curve flattening." Minutes (released late Wed) had "many" wanting to drop easing bias and "majority" warning hikes likely needed if inflation persists. SFRZ6 traded a new low close at 96.10 (-6); SFRM7 closed -6 at 95.99. OIS Dec'26 pushed to 3.84%. Flow had SFRM7/U7/Z7/H8 95.25 put strip paid 97 on 500 — a clear reds/greens downside expression. 0QN6 95.1875 vs SFRZ6 95.375 put spread paid 5.5 on 5k (+Z6) ties to ongoing mid-curve put structures.

**Friday's two-sided builds:** ITC tone was more measured; commentary noted Atlanta Fed Q2 GDPNow revised up to 4.3% and a poor Philly Fed (-0.4 vs 17.8). With Iran headlines two-way, paper added both wings — SR3U6 97.25c +46,809 and SR3Z6 97.00c +24,090 on the topside, SR3N6 95.37p +20,500 and SR3Z6 95.37p +30,459 on the downside. Flow: SFRZ6 96.625/97.125 call spread 1x2 sold 2k at -0.25, SFRU6 condors and put condors stacked at tight widths around 96.00-96.50 — paper monetising the body, owning the tails.
